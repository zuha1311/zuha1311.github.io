Coming Together

An online exhibition to portray the harmony between Southern European migrants and Scottish people


Client: Nikolaos Papadogiannis

Role: UX Researcher, UX Designer

Team: 2 UX Designers, 1 Developers, 1 SEO Specialist



Client Requirements
1. To make the website more interactive for visitors
2. Propose suggestions on how this exhibition will become more easily traceable and searchable on social media
3. To make the website richer in material
Thought Process

❝
Coming Together stands on a very emotional and sensitive ground and therefore it was necessary to ensure that the data elicitation process is carried out without any form of presumptions. Our client wishes for his exhibition to become more “interactive” which can have a lot of meanings for different types of users. Our team, consisting of computer science, marketing and business backgrounds, unanimously decided that in order to understand the meaning of the word “interactive” we needed answers to the following questions:

1. Is “interaction” really the need?

2. How do different groups of people perceive interaction? Is it bright colors or good content or something else?

3. What is the need for people to “interact” with Coming Together?


❞
User Research

A) Gather Domain Knowledge
Domain knowledge was crucial to understand the vocabulary our client mentioned in his project brief, specifically, “brexit”, “mutually-beneficial relationships”,“southern european migrants” and “non-foreign born Scots”. It also helped us understand the goals, the online presence and the overall system architecture.

B) Gather Data Sources
Since our data elicitation will be less rigorous, i.e, having budget and scheduling constraints and we need to perform rapid data elicitation, we decided on the following data sources:

a. Observations of 5 users
b. Interview of the client, a photographer, a southern european migrant, a few Scotts and a product designer.
c. A short questionnaire for Southern European Migrants and the general public.



So what did the users say?




User Interviews
The team and I conducted interviews of the client (since he was of himself of southern european heritage), a photographer who contributes to the exhibition, a few Scottish people and a web designer. Each interview was a semi-structred interview lasting 45 minutes. The main aim was to gain insights from different work roles and to understand the meaning of “interactive” platforms.





Results? Interaction is not the issue.

Apparently, from our analysis we concluded that it was not interaction that was the problem.

It was “engagement”.

As evident from our observations, people did not know where to go or what to do. There is no sense of direction to guide the user to navigate through the website and make them stay. Due to the static nature of the website, users “lose interest” quite quickly. There is no area where users can contribute to the website. Until people are “engaged” with the website, “interaction” will be a secondary issue.

The Moodboard
Our mood board consists of different elements and inspiration such as websites, photographs from Southern European photographers, inspirational words, colors, self-taken pictures, positive quotations, Southern European cultures, websites with an exciting layout, other virtual and in-person exhibitions and unrecommended materials and approach when designing a website.





Scope and defining areas of interest


The word "engagement" was not in our discussion when we started this project until we conducted the interviews and observations. Engagement should be generated by the people who have contributed in building the website such as the photographers, website designers, and the management team. A lot of users mentioned how interesting the theme of this website could be if it could give people the motivation and interest to access it.



In the era of instagram, where content has become non-exhaustive and one can scroll through hundreds of pictures, the amount of photographs on the website is very less which makes the users lose interest rather quickly.
A lot of the interviewees complained about the repetitive nature of the photographs and how the pictures are unable to convey a story that users are looking for. The users complained that the photographs were not "colourful" enough which decreased the engagement and interest drastically for the users.



There is lack of interactivity between the users and the website. As we mentioned earlier, engagement is the fundamental problem of this project since it caused most of the dismay, but interactivity can inspire engagement, which is something the website has failed to accomplish. The website lacks the features of allowing users to leave their presence onto the website, interacting with other visitors of the website including the Coming Together core team. There is also a lack of audio-video aid which makes it difficult for specially-challenged users to interact with the exhibition.
The users for this area of interest mainly include visitors of the website which can be broadly classified based on their intent of visiting i.e, out of curiosity, to research, to contribute or by accidental visit.



While analyzing our interviews, we noticed how most of the participants of our study complained about “too many words”, hence after some research, we landed on the term “Verbose” which means that a particular concept is explained in more words that what is required, which perfectly captures our next area of interest. Even from a research point of view, where too much information is good, most of the content on the website is repetitive which dilutes the purpose. The content becomes overwhelming for the visitors and hence it fails to engage them. We aim to address this by slicing down the content, using structured texts to improve the readability.




Scenario Sketches

A user sees an ad for the online exhibition and is redirected to the website. On arrival, the user finishes the exhibition within the first two minutes and is struggling to find ways to further interact with the website. This will lead to the user not coming back.
A person finds a promotional banner for the exhibition and decides to have a look. On their arrival, they see an overload of content and a lot of infomration. They are also confused in terms of navigation.
This sketch depicts one of our users from the observations. They were unemployed hence were looking for a job. When they opened the website, they instantly saw "Work" and clicked on it but to their dismay, they found pictures of people working together rather than actual jobs. Following this observations, we noticed a lot of people had complained about the confusing terminology being used across the website.
In this particular scenario, a researcher who is studying the dynamics of southern european migrants and Scotts is looking for material. Due to bad SEO, the website does not occur at higher rankings in the google search. Once he finds the website, the lack of imagery and overload of text startles the researcher
This particular scenario considers people with disabilities accessing the website. A photographic exhbition can be delivered via audio narration describing the image.
We made some conceptual sketches for specific problems of the site such as no feedback when the user clicks on something.
A modified version of the website will allow the user to not only view the online exhibition but even contribute to it via uploading any kind of media. This way people will be excited about the theme and will find ways to document the harmony.
This sketch depicts a user that uses the new layout for the exhbition. They see a clear CTA, interact with it and are taken to a gallery view of the exhibition, the user has a chance to view it in a slideshow as well.

Impact and outcome

Our client took our sugesstions into consideration and got them developed. We shadowed a UX designer while they A/B Testing on the site. The results were as follows:

  • The monthlly active users of the website grew from 2% to 7% in 3 months.
  • Time spent on the website increased from 2.7 minutes to more than 10 minutes which shows that people starting interacting with more content on it.
  • Usability tests showed that 75% people did not feel lost and appreciated more media rather than more text.

Reflections

This was my first experience of working in a team which taught me how important diverse views are. This project dealt a lot of with providing recommendations more than actually designing. These recommendartions needed to be backed up with data and research which shows how differently users of a website perceive the journey as compared to designers and developers. We were aiming to do A/B testing with the pages however due this project being part of an academic module did not provide the timeframe for us to do so. A future aspect for this project would be to do the same and see if there any further changes in the MAU.
